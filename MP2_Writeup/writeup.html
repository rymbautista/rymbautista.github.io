Development Process

Static, Interactive, and Loop

The development process started with trying to make a frog. This process started with making one in the corner and getting the basics about shapes and layering. I also implemented a nested for loop to create a pattern of lilypads. After that, I learned more about Positioning and translating them. I ended up making a version of my frog was centered that became the foundation for many of my other sketches. After making the static frog I made the Interactive frog where I was able to use the mouseX and MouseY to draw where one point of a line, tongue, and a circle where to have it look like the tongue is following a fly where the mouse is. Then I moved on to the infinite loop where I was able to repurpose an example where a rectangle was rotating at the center of the page. I positioned my statice frog in the middle and then I created lilypads that surrounded it and rotated around it.

Random

Taking a step away from frogs I made a flower that would change pedal/pot size and shape by utilizing random boundaries bounded to ranges very close to when I first made the flower. This made it so that parts of the flower were in a fixed position but certain points moved around to vary the features of the flowers while keeping the general flower shape.

Audio and Audio Visual

After receiving help from Han and one of the examples for audio I was able to make a piece that would play the whopper song from a currently trending Burger King commercial. When the page is clicked the song will play and a whopper will appear when clicked again would stop the song while leaving only the plate behind. The Audio Visual piece was based on the rainbow keyboard example and reused elements from the infinite loop and interactive frog to have lilypads that would play a note and change color while having a gro stick its tongue out and eat a fly.

Setting up the Gallery

Next, I set up my gallery. I repurposed the code for my home page as it already had a gallery-like layout that would well. I then imported my code and linked everything together.

Adjustments

After implementing my pieces into my website I viewed and made changes to some of the pieces based on how they were displayed on an actual web page as it was different than the web editor preview.

Issues

Learning to draw in P5

The biggest roadblock I faced was getting over the hurdle of drawing in P5 it a while to understand how positions work and constantly looked at the reference sheet to see if I missed any inputs. Although I still struggle with them I have a better understanding of how to draw thanks to practicing.

push and pop

One of the things I did not understand was how push and pop worked, but after watching a youtube video, mentioned in the kudos below. I was able to figure out that push and pop needed to be used one after the other to chunk a piece to be separately affected from other components when for example translating.

Positioning and Sizing

One issue I had when I brought my files outside of the editor was having a real grasp of the sizing as the tiny window in the editor did a good job for most of my pieces some did not do well. It just required me to go back and forth between reloading the code to see how it does in different screen sizes
